Queen Elizabeth Grammar School - Student (2011-2018)

I look back at my secondary school education with great fondness. Here are a few highlights:

While studying at secondary school, I decided to learn how to play the saxophone. After learning the basics, I joined the school orchestra and played in concerts organised by our conductor and joining the school's jazz band soon after made me the coolest kid in the class - or so I thought!
Fast forward a few years and I had become Grade 5 saxophonist, often riffing with my teacher, who herself had her own jazz band. Playing the saxophone has been a fantastic escape for me when I'm stressed, and I would recommend it to anyone!

As someone who has lived near the Lake District for most of their lives, I have a natural love of the great outdoors. The men in my family have a tradition of hiking up Skidaw with their father from a young age, and so my passion for adventure was born when my dad took me on that very trip! After countless more hikes with my family and friends, I have scaled Scafell Pike (England's highest mountain) and it is my dream to one day conquer the Three Peaks.
Much like hiking, running allows me to explore nature whilst also keeping fit. I often run across the Tay Road Bridge when in Dundee (as there's little chance to take shortcuts) and around my home town of Lockerbie when not at university. Discovering new routes and beating my previous times has always been a big motivator, and I have recently been running half marathons.
There's no better way to document the places I've been than through video, and editing these has been a great joy. Transferring the video editing skills that I have developed to my university work and beyond has been incredibly satisfying!

Having a love for adventure led me into taking part in the Duke of Edinburgh Gold Award. My favourite part by far was the expedition during which I climbed Scafell Pike amongst several other mountains within the Lake District. Unsurprisingly it rained everyday of the expedition, but upon reflection, the experience layed the foundation to my stubborn determination. Given the chance, I would enrol in DofE again in a heartbeat, and would highly encourage anyone thinking about it!
